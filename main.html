Meghalaya


Meghalaya, known as the "Abode of Clouds," is a state in northeastern India, characterized by its mountainous terrain, lush forests, and abundant rainfall. It's one of the "Seven Sister States" of India, bordering Assam to the north and Bangladesh to the south. The state's capital is Shillong, also known as the "Scotland of the East," and is renowned for its scenic beauty and hill station charm.

  1. Living Root Bridges: These incredible bridges are made from the aerial roots of rubber trees by the Khasi tribe. The Double Decker Root Bridge in Nongriat is especially famous and requires a scenic trek to reach.
  2. Cherrapunji: Known as one of the wettest places on Earth, Cherrapunji features stunning landscapes, the Nohkalikai Falls (India’s tallest plunge waterfall), Mawsmai Caves, and panoramic viewpoints.
  3. Dawki and Umngot River: The crystal-clear waters of the Umngot River at Dawki near the India-Bangladesh border are a must-see. Boat rides here offer a surreal experience, as boats seem to float on air.
  4. Shillong (Scotland of the East): The capital city offers colonial charm, scenic viewpoints like Shillong Peak and Laitlum Canyons, and the beautiful Ward's Lake and Elephant Falls. It’s also known for its vibrant music scene.
  5. Mawlynnong (Asia’s Cleanest Village): This charming village is renowned for its cleanliness, sustainability practices, and hospitality. Nearby attractions include the Sky View Point and balancing rock formations.

  • Dohneiiong: A traditional Khasi rice dish cooked with pork, aromatic spices, and sometimes blood (optional).
  • Pumaloi: Steamed rice powder dish often served with meat or vegetables.
  • Momos and Smoked Meats: While not exclusive to Meghalaya, the momos here—especially with local meats—are exceptionally good. Also, look out for smoked meats like smoked beef and chicken.
